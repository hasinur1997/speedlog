Your internet slows down every evening — and every time you call your provider, you have
nothing to show for it. Speedlog quietly lives in your menu bar, tracking your real
upload and download speeds and turning them into a clean, minute-by-minute history.

## Landing page sections (short copy blocks)

**Always watching, never in the way.**
Speedlog starts at login and sits in your menu bar showing live download and upload
speeds. Quit it anytime — you're in control.

**History that reads like a story.**
No noisy graphs. Speedlog records your connection as simple time segments:
"10:01 AM – 10:05 AM · ↓ 5.0 MB/s ↑ 1.2 MB/s." Scan a whole day in seconds.

**Find any moment.**
Filter by date, date range, or down to the exact time window. Paginated, fast, tidy —
even with months of records.

**Evidence, not anecdotes.**
One click exports a polished PDF report — your name, the date range, and every record —
ready to attach to an ISP support ticket or send to your employer.

## App Store description (long form)
Speedlog is a lightweight menu bar app that keeps an honest record of your internet
connection.

It measures your real upload and download throughput every second and automatically
groups it into readable time segments, so your history looks like a log, not a
seismograph. Browse it in a fast, filterable table — by date, date range, or a specific
time window — and export any range as a professional PDF report that includes your name
and the reporting period.

Perfect for:
• Documenting slowdowns before you contact your internet provider
• Remote workers who need to show connectivity records
• Anyone curious what their connection actually does all day

Features:
• Live download/upload speed in your menu bar
• Automatic connection history as clean time-range records
• Powerful filters: date, date range, time, and time range
• Paginated reports table built for months of data
• One-click PDF export with a personalized, dated header
• Starts at login; fully stops when you quit — no hidden background process
• Tiny footprint: native app, no accounts, all data stays on your Mac

Privacy: Speedlog measures how much data moves — never what's inside it. No content
inspection, no browsing history, no cloud. Everything is stored locally.

Free version includes live speeds and your last 7 days of history.
Speedlog Pro unlocks unlimited history and PDF export.

## Support / ISP-dispute angle (blog or FAQ snippet)
"How do I complain to my ISP effectively?" Providers respond to specifics. Instead of
'it's slow at night,' send: 'Between July 1–6, my measured throughput dropped below
1 MB/s every day from 8–11 PM — report attached.' Speedlog's PDF export exists for
exactly this sentence.

## Honesty guardrails (do not violate in any copy)
- Say "measures your real throughput/traffic" — never "measures the speed your ISP
  delivers" (that's capacity; requires v1.1 scheduled speed tests).
- Never promise refunds/outcomes from ISPs.
- "No hidden background process" claim depends on quit-stops-tracking behavior (NST-404)
  and KeepAlive=false (NST-901) — keep true.
